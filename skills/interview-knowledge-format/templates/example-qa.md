# About me

> Topic: self-introduction & background
> Use for the opening of any interview.

## Tell me about yourself.

**Q:** Tell me about yourself.

**A:**

I'm a product manager with 4 years building data-heavy B2B tools. Started in analytics, moved into product when I realized the dashboards I was shipping weren't being used — I wanted to fix that root cause.

Most recently I led the agentic-workflow surface at [Company X], shipping the first internal AI assistant adopted by 60% of the org within a quarter.

Now looking for a role where I can keep that focus: AI-native product, real users, real metrics.

> Opener you'd actually say: "Sure — I'm a PM, 4 years, mostly data-heavy B2B."
> Hook: "shipped an internal AI assistant, 60% adoption in a quarter."
> Pivot: "looking for AI-native product work with real users."

## Why are you leaving your current role?

**Q:** Why are you leaving your current role?

**A:**

Two reasons. First, the team I joined to build is wrapping — the agentic AI roadmap I scoped is now in maintenance mode, not net-new. Second, I want to work on a product where AI is the substrate, not a feature.

I left on great terms; my manager wrote me a recommendation, and I'm closing out my last sprint cleanly.

> Two reasons, in order: roadmap maturing → no net-new; want AI-as-substrate.
> Closing line: "manager wrote me a recommendation, sprint closes cleanly."
