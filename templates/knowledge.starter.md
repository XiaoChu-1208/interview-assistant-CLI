# Starter knowledge base

> Interview Assistant RAG expects. See the `interview-knowledge-format` skill

## Tell me about yourself.

**Q:** Tell me about yourself / 请做个自我介绍

**A:**

I'm a [your role] with [N] years of experience, focused on [domain]. Most recently I [what you shipped + the metric]. I'm looking for [the kind of work you want next] because [the genuine reason].

> Opener you'd actually say: "Sure — I'm a [role], [N] years, mostly [domain]."
> One concrete win, with a number.
> One sentence about what you want next.

```tree
▶ Self-intro
├─ ① role + years + domain
├─ ② most recent shipped thing + metric
└─ ③ what you want next + why
```

**A:**

## How do you handle disagreement with engineers?

**Q:** Tell me about a time you disagreed with an engineer / 怎么处理跟工程师的分歧

**A:**

We disagreed about [the specific thing — a feature scope, a tech choice]. I started by re-stating their position back to them so they knew I'd actually heard it.

Then I anchored to the user metric: [the metric we'd both committed to]. We agreed to A/B-test the two options on a small slice of traffic.

The data showed [outcome]. We shipped [decision]. The relationship improved because we'd both signed up to "let the data decide" before we knew the answer.

```tree
▶ Conflict resolution
├─ ① ack: restate their position
├─ ② re-anchor: shared user metric
├─ ③ propose: A/B test
└─ ④ decide: data, not opinion
```

## What's a weakness you're working on?

**Q:** What's your biggest weakness? / 你最大的缺点是什么？

**A:**

I tend to over-index on shipping and under-invest in writing things down. It's bitten me when handing off — onboarding cost is higher than it should be.

What I'm doing about it: I now write a one-page decision doc for every non-trivial product call, and I share it before the meeting, not after. It's slowed me down by maybe 10%, but I've stopped being the bottleneck for the team.

> Real weakness, not a humblebrag.
> Concrete, dated change in behaviour.
> Honest about cost.

## Do you have any questions for us?

**Q:** Do you have questions for me? / 你有什么想问我的？

**A:**

A few — pick whichever's most useful for your time:

1. What's a decision you made in the last quarter you'd make differently in hindsight?
2. Six months in, what would success look like for the person in this role?
3. Where does the team disagree internally — and how do you resolve it?
4. What's the one thing about working here that surprised you?

> Pick 1-2 max in the actual interview. Not a checklist.
